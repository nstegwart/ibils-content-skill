## 0. Doctrine — EXPERIENCE POSITION (non-negotiable)

> Every deck is written from **inside a life someone is living right now** — not from a textbook, not from a market desk, not from a policy memo.

**Angle always = experience.**  
You are not teaching “personal finance concepts.” You are standing in the reader’s Tuesday: the bank app, the 20th of the month, the chat that said “buy the dip,” the coffee that *feels* essential, the empty emergency pot while they still want a trip.

| EXPERIENCE (ON) | LECTURE / LABELS (OFF) |
|-----------------|------------------------|
| Scene you can film | Category title (“Budgeting 101”) |
| “You said… / Month three… / The 20th…” | “It is important to…” |
| Feeling + object + wrong belief | Abstract mechanism with no room |
| What happens in the app / on payday | What a whitepaper defines |
| Closing that fits **this** week’s story | Generic CTA (RUN THE MATH) |

**One-line test:** If a tired worker in London, Lagos, Jakarta remote, or Sydney would **not** feel “that’s me” by slide 2 → rewrite the angle, not just the adjectives.

## 2. How an experience deck moves (slide spine)

1. **Cover** — name the lived trap (account, coffee, dip, empty savings).  
2. **Slide 2** — stay in the room (month three, early twenties, “you said you’d buy the dip”).  
3. **Mid** — mechanism + number **as it hits the body/wallet** (3× spend, payday auto-transfer, living pot empty on the 20th).  
4. **Twist** — wrong belief dies (“not lazy about life — lazy about typing coffee”; “fun allowed — empty isn’t”).  
5. **Close** — short human punch for **this** experience only.

**Voice:** you + contractions always. Friend who has the receipt — not a coach yelling habits.

## 4. Global English (not America-default)

- Travels: Klarna / Afterpay / Clearpay, Revolut / Monzo / Wise, Netflix / Spotify, rent, mortgage ~1/3 take-home, ETF / index fund, BNPL.

- Amounts: universal numbers (“100 a month”, “3 months of spend”) OK; invent no FX.
