# Example carousels — the GOLD STANDARD (study and imitate)

These are REAL human-written IBILS carousels that performed well, written here
in the ENGLISH voice the account now ships in. Before you write a plan, READ
them. Your copy must hit the SAME punch, voice, and rhythm. Do not copy their
topics — copy their *energy and craft*.

What every example below does (do the same):
- Cover = a blunt hook: a fear question, a myth it kills, or a shock number.
- Headlines = claims / warnings / myth-busts, never flat labels. Headlines are
  set in CAPS — that is the display style, not shouting.
- Voice = spoken English, "you", contractions everywhere ("won't", "you're",
  "don't"), confrontational, real emotional stakes. Body copy NEVER shouts in
  caps — the punch comes from the words.
- Lists are uneven and specific. Never three balanced clauses in a row — that
  is the loudest AI tell there is (see content-rules §4.1 BANLIST).
- The carousel is ONE argument built slide by slide.
- The 2nd-last slide ties the lesson to the Ibils app, softly.

## EXAMPLE 1 — insight — "Minimum wage"

- **Cover** — `MINIMUM WAGE: BROKE FOR LIFE?`
- **Content — `FRUGAL LIVING WON'T SAVE YOU`**
  If you're on minimum wage, you can squeeze every last expense and you still
  won't buy a house or retire without panic. Why? Prices climb faster than your
  raise does. Being frugal is good, but it doesn't fix an income that's simply
  too small. So stop cutting — start raising the income: a side hustle, or a
  skill the market actually pays a premium for.
- **Content — `"VICTIM MENTALITY" IS EATING YOU`**
  "Of course I'm broke. I never got the head start they did." Careful — that's
  learned helplessness. The moment you accept your fate, your brain quietly
  stops looking for a way out. Own it if you started at minus. But if you settle
  there, you stay there, and you hand it straight down to your kids.
- **Content — `SAVE ON PAYDAY, NOT ON LEFTOVERS`**
  Don't save what's left over — nothing is ever left over. The day your pay
  lands, move at least 10% into your emergency fund before you touch anything.
  What survives that is your 30 days. Make yourself broke on day one so you're
  not broke at sixty.
- **Content — `LEVELLING UP TAKES 3 THINGS`**
  Change the circle: stop spending every weekend with people who only complain.
  Upgrade what you sell: learn a skill people pay a premium for — data, sales,
  AI tooling. Guard the cashflow: know where the money actually goes, so when an
  opening shows up you can afford to take it.
- **Content (Ibils tie) — `YOU CAN'T LEVEL UP IN THE DARK`**
  It's hard to take a real shot when your own money is a blur. Ibils logs your
  daily cashflow, so you can put your head into the part that matters — making
  the income bigger.
- **Closing** — `START TODAY`

## EXAMPLE 2 — insight — "Saving alone won't make you rich"

- **Cover** — `SAVING ALONE WON'T MAKE YOU RICH`
- **Content — `YOUR SAVINGS ARE QUIETLY SHRINKING`**
  A bank account pays 0–2% a year. Inflation runs 2.5–3.5%. So the money you
  "saved" buys less every year, even though the number on the screen never
  moves. So where should it actually sit?
- **Content — `DEPOSITS: THE SAFEST SHELF`**
  Guaranteed by LPS up to Rp2 billion, paying 3–5% — just barely ahead of
  inflation. The catch: the money is locked until maturity and the interest is
  taxed 20%. Good for cash you genuinely will not touch for a year.
- **Content — `MUTUAL FUNDS: SOMEONE ELSE DRIVES`**
  You don't have to read the market every day — a fund manager does it for you.
  No tax on the gain, and returns run 3–22% depending on the type. Start with a
  money-market fund (the calmest) and only walk toward equity funds when a bad
  month won't make you sell.
- **Content — `STOCKS: BIG RETURN, BIG DROP`**
  The IHSG has returned around 10% a year over the long run, but a single month
  can swing tens of percent either way. It costs you real time to learn. Only
  for money you won't need soon, and only once your emergency fund is already
  full.
- **Content (Ibils tie) — `EMERGENCY FUND FIRST`**
  Before a single rupiah goes into any of this, the emergency fund has to be
  full. Ibils tracks what comes in and what goes out every day, straight from
  your phone — so you know the month you're actually ready.
- **Closing** — `SET TARGETS`

## EXAMPLE 3 — news — "Indonesia's online-loan debt"

(news mode: open with the real sourced number, then unpack it, explain the
jargon, then tell the reader what to check. Cite the source — here, OJK.)

- **Cover** — `ONLINE LOANS IN INDONESIA HIT RP101 TRILLION`
- **Content — `UP 26% IN A SINGLE YEAR`**
  OJK: outstanding online-loan financing reached Rp101 trillion, up 26% in a
  year. More people are leaning on these loans — and not for big things. It's
  bills, groceries, and the hole that opens up before payday.
- **Content — `WHAT DOES "OUTSTANDING" MEAN?`**
  Outstanding = every loan that's still live and not yet paid off. So Rp101
  trillion is the size of the repayment load borrowers are carrying right now,
  this month.
- **Content — `MORE PEOPLE ARE PAYING LATE`**
  OJK puts TWP90 at 4.52%. TWP90 = the share of loans more than 90 days late.
  The industry keeps itself under a 5% ceiling, so 4.52% isn't a fire yet — but
  it's close enough to be an alarm.
- **Content — `YOU SAY YOU'RE FINE. YOU'RE GUESSING.`**
  Most people feel safe because they never actually checked. Without a record
  you don't know what came in, where it went, how much of your instalments are
  still live, or what's genuinely left. That's the gap where a small loan turns
  into a long one.
- **Content (Ibils tie) — `CHECK THIS BEFORE YOU BORROW`**
  Before you take a loan, the question isn't "will it get approved" — it's
  whether your cashflow can still breathe once the instalment lands. Ibils logs
  what you earn and what you already owe, so the call comes from data instead of
  a feeling.
- **Closing** — `CHECK FIRST`

|---|---|
| "Annual work-clothes budget" | flat label, zero hook, niche |
| "Emergency cash saving strategies" | a label, not a hook |

| cover "DOLLAR HITS RP17,500" + body "Rp17,726" | number invented in the hook, contradicts the body |
| "Ibils makes budgeting faster, easier, and more accurate" | balanced triplet — the loudest AI-written tell there is |
| "Unlock financial freedom and take control of your finances" | marketing fluff + empty payoff; says nothing real |
| "In today's fast-paced world, money moves quickly" | LLM throat-clearing — start on the point |
| "you NEED to stop doing this" (in a body sentence) | uppercase shouting in body copy — caps are for headlines |
| "the rupiah reportedly weakened" | hedge word — say it direct, with the source name |
| "Forward your receipt from email or WhatsApp" | claims a feature that does not exist — WhatsApp forwarding ONLY (see ibils-app.md) |
