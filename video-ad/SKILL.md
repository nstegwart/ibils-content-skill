# Ibils Video Ad — cinematic vertical film

A 30s vertical ad where **every frame is a real film frame**, not an illustration with a caption
pasted on. codex composes each still *including its typography*; grok animates it; ffmpeg cuts it
to a beat grid and scores it.

This file is the accumulated cost of getting it wrong. Read the LAWS before generating anything.

## 1. Copy comes from Gemini

All final copy (script lines, CTA, captions) is generated by **Gemini** via the `ibils-browser` agent.
Other models draft; Gemini writes the shipped sentence. This is a standing owner rule.

**Banned:** "financial freedom", "100% gratis", any promise of a feature that is not shipped.
Check every claim against `references/ibils-app.md` — that registry is the ONLY source of truth for
what the app does. It has been wrong before (it listed email forwarding, which is **NOT shipped**),
and a 54-agent QC pass validated against it and shipped the lie anyway. **The registry can be wrong.
When a claim is load-bearing, ask the owner.**

## 2. THE LAWS

These are not style preferences. Each one is a bug that shipped.

### LAW 1 — Typography is BAKED, never overlaid
codex composes the type INTO the frame as part of the picture. It is a typographer and a director
working together, not a caption bar.
- ffmpeg painting a transparent PNG overlay comes out **opaque black**.
- A caption box / banner / subtitle slab reads as cheap. If type needs separation, earn it with
  composition and value.
- Type sits **inside the film's grade** — warm cream, never pure white; if light falls across it,
  the light affects it.

### LAW 2 — De-bait the plate, or the video model hallucinates text
grok **compulsively invents letters onto any flat blank printable surface.** On a film about paper
receipts this failed 6 of 9 shots.

Every sheet of paper in a plate must ALREADY be destroyed as a writing surface: crumpled, curled
tight, folded under, torn, soaked with the ink bled into grey clouds, edge-on, or half-dissolved.
**No blank flat paper. No empty screens. No clean printable panels.** A single flat blank rectangle
anywhere ruins the shot.

A phone may only **emit light** — never show a UI, dashboard, chart or number.

### LAW 3 — Restraint is what keeps the character intact
grok morphs the mascot after ~3s *if the motion prompt lets it*. Observed: a **scepter** grown at
3.6s, the **cape melting into a gown** at 3.5s, our baked caption **dissolving** entirely.

The fix is not trimming — it is the prompt. Ship this in every motion prompt, verbatim:

> Extremely SLOW, SUBTLE, restrained cinematic motion — a gentle, almost imperceptible drift.
> The character MUST NOT change shape, costume, pose or identity. He grows NO new props (no scepter,
> no staff, no orb). His cape stays a cape. NO new objects appear. NO text is added, altered or
> removed ANYWHERE in the frame. Only light, air, dust and a slow camera may move.

With that, clips stay clean across the **full 6.04s** — including baked type and a composited app
screenshot held perfectly static. Verified at 4.5s and 5.8s.

### LAW 4 — Never stretch time. Cut the clip to length.
grok emits 6.04s / 145 frames / 24fps. The longest shot needed is ~4.4s.
**Cut the grok clip at exactly the shot length. No `setpts`. 100% real motion.**

The first cut only trusted grok's 0–2.8s window and stretched it to 4.4s — **~64% of delivered frames
were synthesised just to fill time.** That is what the owner saw as "ga smooth". It was never the fps
number.

Then, and only then, upconvert 24 → 60fps:
```
minterpolate=fps=60:mi_mode=mci:mc_mode=aobmc:me_mode=bidir:vsbmc=1
```
That only invents frames between REAL samples 41.7ms apart — a completely different operation from
stretching 67 frames across 105. Verified clean on rain ripples (worst case for motion estimation).

### LAW 5 — `zoompan` is the judder. Never use it.
It quantises zoom to integer pixels and steps visibly. **grok's motion IS the motion.** Do not add a
synthetic Ken-Burns push on top of it.

### LAW 6 — The light arc is measurable, not a vibe
"Habis gelap terbitlah terang" is a **curve you assert in CI**, not a mood.

Render the plates on an explicit rising light curve, then measure mean luminance and **fail the build
if it does not climb**. The shipped ad:

```
0.158  ->  0.080 at "Otak lo nyerah"  ->  0.422 at dawn      = 5.3x climb
             ^ rock bottom lands ON the moment of surrender
```

The floor must land on the emotional floor. A film that is uniformly dark has no payoff.

### LAW 7 — Assert every shot, or a truncated one ships silently
`2>/dev/null` on an ffmpeg build step will hide a shot that came out short — it quietly shortens the
film and breaks beat lock. (Caught a 2.79s shot that should have been 4.35s: ffmpeg read the clip
while grok was still flushing it to disk.)

**Assert per shot: duration, and that it actually moves** (frame-delta RMSE > 0.004).

## 3. THE SCORE

### LAW 8 — Never stitch two recordings
Matching key and tempo is **necessary but not sufficient**. Two recordings = two pianos, two rooms,
two reverb tails — and the join lands mid-phrase. A butt-join between recordings is a **cut, not a
transition**, and no crossfade hides it.

**Find ONE cue that already performs dark → light.** Then the composer's transition IS the
transition, and it is smooth by construction — there is no seam to smooth.

`scripts/video/find_arc.py` scans every 30s window of a candidate library and scores its *internal*
arc (brightness lift, energy rise, and how CONCENTRATED the turn is at the target mark).

### Tempo lock is a hard requirement
The cue's BPM must be the cut grid's BPM **or an exact multiple** (90 / 180). Off-grid music is the
owner's "music ga sync sepanjang jalan". A cue with a better arc but the wrong tempo is the wrong cue
— that trade has already been made and it was wrong.

### Loudness traps (both cost a rebuild)
- **`alimiter` defaults to `level=true`, which applies auto MAKE-UP GAIN** — the limiter makes it
  *louder*, past your limit. Always `alimiter=limit=0.891:level=disabled`.
- **`loudnorm` single-pass TP is only an estimate** and AAC overshoots on top. Two-pass measure, then
  apply with `linear=true`.
- **Measure true peak PER CHANNEL.** Decoding with `-ac 1` downmixes L+R and can sum past 1.0, which
  reads as clipping that is not there. That false alarm cost a wasted rebuild.

### Licensing
Everything must be **Pixabay Content License** (royalty-free, commercial OK, **no attribution
required**) or equivalent. Record it in `MUSIC_LICENSE.md` **before** using the track — title, artist,
URL, licence, and *why* it was chosen. Paid advertising for a commercial product is the strictest
normal case.

> **Do not "compose it yourself" as a shortcut.** Without fluidsynth/timidity/a real sample library
> you get General-MIDI piano, which sounds cheaper than the free orchestral recordings. That is a
> downgrade, not a solution.

## 4. THE BEAT GRID

Everything is cut to **90 BPM** (beat = 0.6667s). Shot lengths are whole beats.

```
shot  beats  running        act
 1      4     2.667         DARK
 2      5     6.000
 3      4     8.667
 4      6    12.667
 5      5    16.000   <-- THE TURN. beat 24 = downbeat of bar 7.
 6      5    19.333         LIGHT
 7      6    23.333
 8      6    27.333
 9      5    30.667
```

**Transitions.** The descent (1→4) cuts **hard** — urgent, accumulating. The turn and the dawn
**dissolve** (0.25–0.40s). Shot 5→6 gets the longest dissolve: it is the hinge.

**Dissolves must not break beat lock.** Cut each segment LONGER by exactly its outgoing transition;
xfade eats the surplus, so the cut still lands on the downbeat.

## 5. PIPELINE

```
1. SCRIPT      Gemini (via ibils-browser). Check every claim against references/ibils-app.md.
2. PLATES      codex gpt-5.5 imagegen. ONE SESSION PER IMAGE, ALL PARALLEL.
               Attach the 4 himel-pose refs from ../assets/ to lock identity.
               Type baked in (LAW 1). Plate de-baited (LAW 2). Light arc explicit (LAW 6).
3. QC PLATES   LOOK AT THEM. Anatomy, spelling, no invented features, arc rising.
4. ANIMATE     grok image_to_video, 6s, 720p. Restrained motion prompt (LAW 3). One worker per clip,
               in parallel — one worker doing N sequential videos stalls.
5. QC CLIPS    Sample each at 0.2 / 2.8 / 4.5 / 5.8s. Character intact? Type intact? No new props?
6. ASSEMBLE    Cut to length (LAW 4). No zoompan (LAW 5). 60fps. Beat grid. Assert (LAW 7).
7. SCORE       One cue with a built-in arc (LAW 8). Tempo-locked. Licence recorded.
```

**codex dispatch** (one session per image, all parallel — the owner has a $200 account, there is no
session budget to conserve and **no account rotation**):
```
codex exec -m gpt-5.5 -c model_reasoning_effort="high" \
  --dangerously-bypass-approvals-and-sandbox --skip-git-repo-check \
  -i <ref1> -i <ref2> -i <ref3> -i <ref4> -C <outdir> -
```
prompt on stdin.

**grok dispatch:** `/Users/user/.agents/tools/grok-worker.sh <slug> <cwd>`, prompt on stdin.
Prepend `SKIP_RESTATEMENT` or the worker burns its turn restating the task.

> **Trap:** do not `&`-background a worker inside an already-backgrounded shell — the process group
> gets reaped the moment the shell returns, and the worker dies with only its log header written
> *while the tool still reports exit 0*. Launch it in the foreground, or `&` + `wait`.

> **A worker's own WORKER_RESULT is not proof.** One reported `IN PROGRESS` and exited; the mp4s on
> disk were byte-identical to the stale ones. Verify artifacts by mtime against a cutoff taken AFTER
> dispatch — same-day mtimes pass a naive check.

## 6. LOOK

Shared with the carousel — see `../references/styles.md`.

**Cinematic manga noir.** A frame from a high-end animated short, never an illustration.
- The figure lives INSIDE a staged environment: foreground, midground, background, real depth.
- **Motivated** lighting from a source that exists in the scene.
- **Integration:** the ink figure and the environment share ONE rendering language — the same
  halftone/grain runs THROUGH figure AND ground. Real contact shadows. Never a pasted cut-out.
- Film grain, print texture, slight off-register ink.

**Palette.** Deep Ibils green `#0E3B33` = night ambient. Cream `#FBF6E9` = light.
Amber `#F2A93B` = **one** point of meaning per frame. Never decoration.

**Himel.** Identity locked by the 4 refs in `../assets/himel-pose-*.png`: small child-king, bangs over
one eye, **thin pointed line-crown** (not chunky/jewelled), scarf, long tunic, puffy trousers, tall
cuffed boots, a cape that always reads as a **cape** (never a gown).
**BANNED: scepter, orb, staff, any fantasy prop.** The refs show one; it is retired.

**End card.** Use the real app: composite `../assets/closing-phone.png` (the Ibils splash) into a void
the plate deliberately reserves for it. Have codex compose the scene with the centre-right left
**completely empty** — then drop the phone in. Compositing the phone on top of a finished frame
punched it through the CTA text and the owner called it "rusak total".
**No App Store / Play badges** — the ad points at the landing page, not a store.
