# LOOK — `kansas-doc` (castless documentary)

**No mascot. No cast. No character sheet.**

This look exists because the story format needed it and Himel could not do it. The Heartland film is
about a man who took his daughter's college fund, destroyed his neighbours' bank, and showed the
Federal Reserve a balance that never existed. A manga child-king with a crown standing in that frame
would make it a joke. **The subject sets the register, and this subject is not funny.**

## The rendering language

**A photograph, not an illustration.** Grainy, un-stylised, uncomfortably plain — the visual language
of documentary and archive, not of a brand.

- Shot on film. Real grain, real lens. Slight halation on the highlights.
- **Natural, motivated light only.** Prairie sun through a window. A phone screen in a dark kitchen.
  A fluorescent tube in a bank. Nothing is lit for beauty.
- **Static or nearly static frames.** The camera does not editorialise. It watches.
- Composition is plain and centred, or plainly off-centre. **No dutch angles, no drama in the
  framing.** The facts are already dramatic; the camera adding drama is the camera not trusting them.
- **NO FACES IN CLOSE-UP.** Backs, hands, silhouettes, figures at distance. We are not casting an
  actor as a real convicted man. This is also a legal and ethical line, not just a style choice.

## The palette — Ibils, kept, but drained

The brand colours survive so the film still reads as ours, but they are **desaturated toward
documentary**:

| | |
|---|---|
| **deep green `#0E3B33`** | the night ambient, the dark kitchen, the shadow. Present but muted. |
| **cream `#FBF6E9`** | wheat, prairie light, paper. This is the dominant tone by day. |
| **amber `#F2A93B`** | **ONE point per frame, and it is almost always the phone.** The screen is the only warm light in the film, and it is the thing that destroys him. That is not decoration — that is the whole story in one colour. |

The phone glow being the only amber in the film is the single strongest art-direction decision here.
Do not spend amber anywhere else.

## Hard constraints

- **NO TEXT ANYWHERE IN FRAME.** No signage, no bank name, no readable documents, no phone UI. This
  is inherited law (the video model hallucinates letters onto any flat printable surface), and it is
  doubled here: a fabricated bank name or a fabricated balance would be a fabricated FACT in a film
  whose entire authority is that every fact is real.
  - A phone may **EMIT LIGHT ONLY**. Never a screen with numbers on it.
  - A document may appear **only** face-down, edge-on, or too far to read.
- **No real person's likeness.** No portrait that could be taken for the actual man.
- No lens flares, no colour grading toward "cinematic teal-orange", no drone shots.

## The world

Elkhart, Kansas. Population two thousand. High plains. Wheat, grain elevators, a low brick main
street, pickup trucks, a chain-link fence, a church with a plain white steeple. A kitchen table at
2am. A farmyard. It is flat, wide, and unglamorous, and the sky is enormous.

**The visual thesis:** an ordinary place, in ordinary light, where something ordinary-looking
happened — a man on his phone at a kitchen table — that took everything from everyone around him.
The horror is that none of it looks like horror.
